WINDOWS APP BUILDING



MICROSOFT INNOVATION CENTRE, Dronacharya College of Engineering, Gurgaon organized an APP BUILDING WORKSHOP ON MICROSOFT AZURE in the college premises in which more than 550 apps were made by the students. Dronacharya being the Microsoft innovation centre facilitated the students with a platform that helps them to develop skills and provides support to work on innovative ideas. The mega event was held from 15th-19th November 2014 under guidance from senior trainers provided by Microsoft along with participation of the innovative minds from Dronacharya.

Day 1:

First day of the training session was for the faculty members of Dronacharya. It was conducted by Mr. Jagdish Sasala. He introduced various Microsoft technologies to our faculty members. He also demonstrated many apps and explained the details of the process for building apps. He discussed and briefed about Windows Azure and told how it is beneficial for the students. It was a very interactive and brain storming session.

Day 2:

The second day of the event was divided into 2 sessions: first a seminar and a hands on session conducted by the experts MR. JAGDISH SASALA, MR.SUSHIL KAPADIA AND MR. ROMIL BHEDA . The day began with a seminar on the subject “APP BUILDING”. . This seminar provided participants with all the tools they needed to establish a vibrant, efficient and conducive learning environment. The trainers shared and demonstrated all the steps needed in building a good app. The students were then directed to various labs where they applied all the concepts taught in the first session. From leveraging folders to open channels of communication, Simple apps using buttons and adding audio files, Sites to share information with the community, and Forms to collect more, better and faster information, students build various apps. This was followed by a brief round of question-answers session. It was overall a very interactive session and was very well received by the audience.

On the whole, Day 2 was a complete package for the students to facilitate them with how to CREATE and Upload an APP and what tools and technology can be used to bring their idea to the ground level.

Day 3:

The 3rd day of APP BUILDING WORKSHOP ON AZURE i.e. 18TH NOVEMBER’ 14 saw active participation by students of all the branches of the college. The basic idea behind the event was to bring out the innovative ideas from the mind of the students and provide them the opportunity to explore its application that could be applicable in the real life . The enthusiasm of the students was manifold on day two. The second day was in continuation to the first day event. The second session was on how to add Animation and Graphic designing in Azure Apps .A total of 420 apps has been uploaded on Windows Store and it is still on.

Day 4:

Though the workshop was scheduled to be a three day workshop but looking at the enthusiasm of the students it was extended for one more day.19th November’14 the fourth and final day of the workshop was convened by Mr. Jagdish Sasala. It was on how to navigate in your App. Final day of the workshop was quiet satisfactory as it was attended by the students from all the branches. They were keen to learn this App building process and were happy with the learning so far. There were certain new things that were taught by the convener. He explained on the subject that how to add multipage and grid view to the App i.e. how to add images and text to the application and scroll them u p. The second half of the session was on how to Implement Static App Dynamically; how to make changes in your online App. The three days workshop witnessed the active participation of the students of all the branches and helped them to enhance their knowledge and think out of the box because “EXCELLENCE IS THE CONTINOUS PROCESS NOT AN ACCIDENT”.

Lastly the event was concluded with the motivational words of Mr. Bhaskar Pramanik, Chairman at Microsoft India. He discussed various achievements, roadmaps and new technologies introduced by Microsoft. Shared below are the details of the speech delivered by the dignitaries.

      Satya Nadella on his 'surreal' journey

      Nadella, who is only the third CEO that Bill Gates-founded Microsoft has had in its nearly four-decades-long history, said businesses were looking forward to an accelerated growth phase in India and technology could play a pivotal role in spurring that growth. "Growth will need computing infrastructure on a massive scale," he said.

      Hyderabad-born Nadella, who studied engineering at Manipal University and was part of the early wave of engineers who moved to the US, said his journey from "home here in India to Microsoft" where he has spent 22 years was "incredible and humbling". "My advice to those who are pursuing careers in technology is to really think about the work you're driving and why it matters. Believe that you can make it happen and go after it with boldness and tenacity. And remember that success doesn't happen overnight, be persistent and ask yourself what you can do to push to the next level each day," said Nadella, who is a cricket buff and loves running. He says about his journey” When I think back, I never grew up thinking that I'll go west of Mumbai and then I land up in Wisconsin.

      Believe me, my journey has not been a simple journey of progress. There have been many ups and downs and it is the choices that I made at each of those times that have helped shape what I have achieved”. He continues that as a youth prepare to change the world, the opportunity before you is unparalleled. All of us represent a multi-disciplinary approach to innovation - and that will change the future. The mobile first, cloud first is a very rich canvas for innovation - it is not the device that is mobile, it is the person that is mobile.

      How can we create platforms, how can we be the tools provider so that others can realise their potential? Today we launched our commercial cloud services The energy you create around you is perhaps going to be the most important attribute -- in the long run EQ trumps IQ. Without being a source of energy for others very little can be accomplished. Every opportunity I got I took it as a learning experience. You have to fall in love with what you do because everything else then becomes so easy. Most importantly it is perhaps the best way to find meaning in what you do...

      Lastly he ended his session with a Quotes Mahatma Gandhi - Live everyday as if it your last day but learn as if you are going to live forever...

      Union Minister Smriti Irani says Satya Nadella is a difficult speaker to top

      She says “We are anxious today because there is a huge expectation from all of you (the youth).We recognize that expectation and it is the youngsters of this country who gave us this mandate - this mandate is your mandate Satya spoke of IQ and EQ - I bring in the GQ component - the governance component.         Microsoft Cloud OS Roadshow



        On 22nd February 2014, Ms. Sarita, Assistant Professor from Dronacharya College of Engineering, Gurgaon, CSE Department, attended a one- day Microsoft Session entitled as “Microsoft Cloud OS Roadshow” held at Microsoft Cooperation Pvt. Ltd, Gurgaon. Event was organized by Mr Tanmay Kapoor (Community Support lead at Microsoft).

        The Cloud OS Roadshow was a full day event hosted by Microsoft MVPs and Community members which lets the participants learn about Windows Azure and its features. Topics covered in this event were: Breakthrough Data Performance Platform, Virtualization, Building Modern Apps and Windows Azure, Introduction to Cloud Computing and Windows Azure Overview, Windows Azure Compute – IaaS, Websites and Cloud Services, Windows Azure Storage – SQL Database, Blob Storage, Table Storage.

        The Main Aim of attending this event is to take a step ahead to get in touch with the real time IT environment, Interaction with MVP’s and other IT cooperate people such as Accenture and Cleaver clues to know how exactly the processing in a business takes place. Students from CSE Department Mr.Bibhuti Bhushan (CSE-3rd Year), Mr. Puneet Bhalla(CSE-3rd Year) Mr.Sahil (CSE-2nd Year) and Mr. Vikas Verma (CSE-2nd Year) also attended this Microsoft Session.

        Microsoft’s Most Valuable Professional (MVP’s) Mr.Gaurav Mantri, Mr. Sharabpreet Singh, Mr. Shaantanu and Mr.Dhanjay Kumar took control of the Session.

        First session:- The very first session of the event was handled by Mr Gaurav Mantri (Founder, Cynapta Software, and Windows Azure MVP) who helped audience to get close to the cloud world through Azure thing. He gave basic information of Cloud computing and its benefits. Speaker also put lights on Cloud as OS.

        Second Session: The second session was headed by Mr Shaantanu Kaushik (Windows Expert-IT pro, Microsoft MVP) who took a step further by telling the concept of Hyper V and its latest versions

        Third Session: This session was handled by Mr Sarabpreet Singh Anand (SQL Server Consultant) who told the new trend in Backend Designing that includes the concept of Databases.

        Last session The last session of the day was handled by Mr Dhananjay Kumar (working at Telerik & Microsoft MVPs) who told about “Windows Azure mobile services as Backend service layer”. At the end of Session, Speakers gave “Microsoft goodies” to the audience on the basis of lucky draw. Event was very informatics and interactive. Faculty member & Students gained a lot of knowledge with this Cloud Roadshow.

            Microsoft Ideation Festival

            Dronacharya College of Engineering, Gurgaon organized Microsoft Ideation Festival – 2014, on 22nd Jan, 2014 and initiated Microsoft Innovation Centre in the campus which would help in accelerating skills and provide support in developing new ideas. It will also help students in nurturing and harboring their Innovations, thus, enacting their ideas into products and fostering entrepreneurship resulting in financial gains also.


            42 students from various departments have participated in the MS Ideation Festival and presented their ideas on various topics like Floating display, Colors of India, Virtual Maps, the Eye-ability Maker for the disabled people, Delay Trauma etc. The idea behind this fest was to encourage the young researchers and student community with their innovative ideas, which are more relevant to contemporary day society yet innovative enough to reflect the mindset of today’s highly creative young generation.


            All the ideas presented by student participants were pronounced by the steering committee. The candidates were evaluated on four main parameters namely, Problem statement, Approach used in solving the problem, Innovation & Feasibility. Top 15 ideas delivered by students have received a Certificate of Achievement from the institution.
