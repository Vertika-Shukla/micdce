Tech - Summit 2016, Hainan, China



Microsoft Innovation Center, Hainan organized Tech Summit 2016 on 30th November 2016 at Hotel Langham, Haikou, China. Mrs. Dimple Saproo, HOD - ECE and Mrs. Megha Goel, HOD - IT & CSIT attended the summit as Guests of Honor. The summit invited guests from Hong Kong, Taiwan, Singapore, India and other countries. The summit also included officials from Hainan provincial government, Taiwan Affairs Office of Hainan Province, Industry and Information Technology Office, Hainan Provincial Foreign Affairs Office, Sanya Municipal Science and Technology. The event focused on the policy "All the Way along the Way", that is, how all the MICs worldwide can collaborate and help each other in the development of the society by the means of spreading awareness and educating young lot towards the new technologies and advancements. The event was jointly organized by the Hainan Provincial Industry and Information Technology Department and Microsoft Innovation Centre, Hainan. The summit aimed to further implement the national "Internet +" and “public entrepreneurship and innovation” strategy, to highlight international development in view of the Internet Industry, and promote the Internet Industry in Hainan and Taiwan, Hong Kong, Singapore, India and other countries. The event started with the welcome speech of Mr. She Yongxu, CEO, Hainan Microsoft Innovation Center. He warmly greeted all the Guests of Honor. It was followed by the introductory speech of Mr. Chen Wanxin, Deputy Director, Hainan Provincial Department of Industry and Information Technology. He focused on the aids and help provided by the Government in the development of MIC, Hainan and the students enrolled under it. The introductory speech was followed by the panel discussion on the agenda strategic development of the industry, new spark for the local enterprises and the Internet entrepreneurs to bring a wealth of experience in the feast of industry experience. The key speakers in the panel discussion were Hong Kong Cyberport CEO Mr. Lin Xiangyang, Neihu Technology Park Development Association Mr. Ma Ji Jie, Director of Taipei, Taiwan Gold To Network CEO Mr. Guo, Singapore Island Branch co-founder Zhu Feng Hao, Mr. Bao Xuan Cheng and Ms. Megha Goel, MIC Coordinator, Dronacharya College of Engineering, India. The summit was ended with the focus on "along the way" strategic linkage development as the core guidance, form a cooperative mechanism across the globe considering Microsoft's innovation center in Hainan as one of the carrier, the transformation of personnel, technology, market advantages, cooperation for the island to provide a high - quality information technology docking platform. In the IT / promote the Internet industry in the three-round cooperation, resource sharing, mutual benefit and win-win. The detailed report of the summit can also be found at the following link: http://hainanmic.com/news_page.aspx?id=2106&cid=5

    MIC Global Summit 2016



    MIC, Shaanxi hosted Microsoft Innovation Centre (MIC) Global Summit - 2016 organized by Microsoft (China) Pvt. Ltd at Xian Sheraton, Xi’an, Shaanxi, People ’s Republic of China from 30th October 2016 to 4th November 2016. Rahul Satyaprakash Rana, student of CSE Department, Dronacharya College of Engineering, Gurgaon attended the Summit. The MIC Global Summit was attended by MIC Director/Managers from around the world. The MIC Summit included the “The Belt and Road International Collaborative Innovation Incubation Development Forum” which was attended people around the globe and from the Chinese Government and UNESCO. Day 1 Welcome and Opening Remarks The MIC Global Summit was started by Mr. Ed Steidl, Global Program Manager, MICs with an introduction to the 4.0 Industrial Revolution and how the industry over the world are changing through the Cloud Computing and Big Data Analytics and taking the world through Industrial Revolution 4.0. Industrial Revolution 4.0 & Impacts on Society Dr. Simon Wilkie, Chief Economic Policy Strategist gave an insight on how Microsoft Corporation is helping and supporting the Industrial Revolution 4.0 and how this would impact the society and employment in the near future by studying the recent trends and how the ICT industry is changing the scenario of employment. Introduction to Chinese Market and Microsoft China Mr. Srikanth Raju, General Manager, Developer Experience & Evangelism, Greater China, gave information on how Microsoft is tackling with the resources needed for the Industrial Revolution 4.0 and the policies that are adopted so that the company can be a driving force for this revolution for the Cloud Services and Data Analytics. Introduction to One Belt One Road Strategy Mr. Jin Jun, Director, MIC Shaanxi, China gave information how the government of People’s Republic of China is coming up with new programs to boost the industrial Revolution in China. Such a program that was introduced was “One Belt One Road” Strategy. Workshop on Invention Cycle Ms. Natasha Chornesky, Program Manager, Microsoft Research Outreach, gave a workshop on “Invention Cycle”. She gave an insight how innovation is different from creativity, how to create new idea for a successful business and how to work in team to maximize the output from the existing team. The Belt and Road Innovation forum The Belt and Road International Collaborative Innovation Incubation Development Forum, was attended by delegates from Chinese Central and Provincial Government Members, UNESCO and MIC’s from around the world. The forum discussed how innovation and incubation of new ICT start - ups can help to boost the economies around the Old Silk Route stretched from China to countries in the continent of Europe. The panellist also discussed how the education system can be changed to make the students of professional courses market ready right from their graduation from the Universities. Day 2 Lean Start - up Machine Workshop The Day 2 of MIC Global Summit consisted of the Lean Start-up Machine Workshop. The workshop consisted of how to make teams for new start-ups and how come out with plans and idea for new start-ups that have high success rate rather than just coming out of ideas that have very high failure rate (98%). The workshop also had two “Get Out of Building” activity for customer interviews which were carried out at Muslim Street Market, Near Drum Tower, Xian, Shaanxi, PRC. Day 3 MSFT Accelerator Workshop The MSFT Accelerator workshop was delivered by Mr. Alec Saunders, Senior Director, Microsoft Accelerator and Didem Un Ates, Partnerships Director, Microsoft Ventures. The workshop focused on how level-2 start-up can ensure the scaling of their organization and how to select the team members and how to obtain funding and how much funding should be obtained from the investors. Day 4 Visit to MIC Shaanxi The visit to MIC Shaanxi is hosted by Provincial Government of Shaanxi and was consisted of a short video presentation about the achievement and upcoming projects and how the MIC Shaanxi is helping the economy of People’s Republic of China to grow and prosper in the ICT Industry. Trip to Terra Cotta Warriors, Xian, Shaanxi, PRC The Trip to Terra Cotta Warriors was organized by MIC Shaanxi. The Terracotta Army is a collection of terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. It is a form of funerary art buried with the emperor in 210 - 209 BCE and whose purpose was to protect the emperor in his afterlife. The summit was very informative and highlighted the importance of technology at every level of education. It was a good experience for the participants to be a part of the summit.

                Microsoft SQL Server Geeks Event



                Microsoft organized “SQL Server Geeks” to introduce the SQL Server 2016 features & specifications at Microsoft Corporation (India) Pvt., Gurgaon, on 5th March 2016. Five Students, Vikash Kumar (17170), Yogesh Vats (17177), Kashika Grover (17064), Kunal Pandey (17071) and Lokesh Sharma (17075) from Department of Computer Science & Engineering, Dronacharya College of Engineering, Gurgaon attended the event.
                The event was divided into seven sessions by six eminent speakers : Mr. Amit R S Bansal, from SQL Maestros, Mr. Aneek Arora from Microsoft, Mr. Ahmad Osama from SQL Maestros, Rahul Dutta from Microsoft, Mr. Sarabpreet Singh Anand from SQL Server Geeks and Mr. Prince Rastogi from SQL Server Geeks. The topics that were discussed in various sessions were : New features in the SQL Server 2016, Leveraging Microsoft BI Solution for Data Insights, SQL Server Cardinality Estimation, and Resource Governor.
                Session 1: Leveraging Microsoft BI Solution for Data Insights by Mr. Aneek Arora
                In his session Aneek Arora told integrated view of redefined Microsoft BI platform Strategy to cover integrated view of entire suite of capabilities. He explained about the Microsoft solution that are helpful in delivering superior capabilities while leveraging both on-premise and cloud offerings.
                Session 2: SQL Server Cardinality Estimation by Mr. Amit R S Bansal
                Mr. Bansal explained how the SQL Query Optimizer produces those awesome execution plans. There was also a demo session that shows the magic and complexity behind cardinality estimator of SQL Server.
                Session 3: Multidimensional Modelling by Mr. Ahmad Osama
                “MOLAP” is discussed in the third session by Mr. Ahmad Osama. In his one hour demo session, he explained, to model data with MOLAP. Mr. Osama also explained to define data sources, dimensions, attributes, relationships, hierarchies and cubes with SQL Server Data tools.
                Session 4: In-Memory Database Technologies by Mr. Rahul Dutta
                Mr. Dutta explained “In memory database (IMDBs)” which is also called as main memory. He told that applications where response time is critical, such as those running telecommunications network equipment and mobile advertising networks, use main-memory databases. IMDBs have gained a lot of traction, especially in the data analytics space, due to its multi-core processors that can address large memory and less expensive RAM.
                Session 5: SQL Server Wait Stats – A Magical Wand to Solve Performance Issues by Mr. Sarabpreet Singh Anand
                SQL Wait Statistics was explained by Mr. Sarabpreet Singh Anand. He told SQL Wait statistics can be the best solution for problems regarding performance issues or finding the root cause of the delay, as it knows about the performance issue and the underlying cause, it’s just that we need to ask SQL and understand its language.
                Session 6: Things That You Don’t Know About SQL Server by Mr. Prince Rastogi
                Mr. Prince Rastogi in his session told few very interesting aspects of SQL Server database engine that even Google cannot find.
                Session 7: Resource Governor for IO by Mr. Amit R S Bansal
                Mr. Amit Bansal demonstrated how to implement Resource Governor to configure resource limits in a comprehensive manner including IO and guarantee predictable performance for different tenants. In addition to this he also explained, enhancements to Extended Events framework and disk specific counters that have been added SQLServer:Resource Pool Stats.
                Microsoft SQL Server Geeks was really a knowledgeable and fruitful event for the participants.
